35MM FILM TRAVEL EVENING

"The sea didn't sound like the ocean we knew back home. It was a rhythmic pulse, a heartbeat against the stones of the harbor."

We sat on the warm stone wall until the sun dipped completely below the horizon, watching the sky transform from a dusty gold to this impossible, vibrant indigo. This is the color I remember most—the blue hour that seems to exist only in memories and old postcards.

Everything felt slower then. No phones were buzzing, no one was rushing to capture the perfect shot for an audience. We were just there, inhaling the salt air and the scent of fried calamari drifting from the trattoria behind us.

I remember the way the light caught the peeling pastel paint of the buildings—pinks and oranges that felt like they had been sun-bleached for centuries. It was the first time I understood that silence wasn't the absence of sound, but the presence of peace.
